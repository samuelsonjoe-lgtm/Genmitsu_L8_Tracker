## 8. Provenance and frozen-snapshot assessment

`tabletopTrayProvenanceSnapshot` captures, per component, `resultId`/`settingId`/`evidenceId` plus a `snapshot` containing the cloned `rawResult`, `fitContext`, `setting.machine`, and `evidenceStage` — genuinely frozen via `promotionClone` at promotion time, stored inside `candidate.fitContext.componentProvenance` (which becomes the persisted Library evidence's `fitContext`) **and** written back onto the raw tray record (`finalizeTabletopTrayPromotion` sets `raw.componentProvenance=...`). **Verified this is self-sufficient, not a live dependency**, both by direct reading and live: after promoting, I deleted the underlying T2/T3A live raw records in a fixture-driven check (the fixture's own "later component deletion does not rewrite promoted complete-tray evidence" assertion, independently re-run and passing) — the already-promoted `fitContext.componentProvenance` and its JSON serialization were byte-identical before and after the deletion, and the raw tray record's own `componentProvenance.shell/storage.snapshot` remained populated. **This correctly matches the architecture review's decision**: the frozen snapshot preserves historical truth regardless of later component deletion, while the eligibility gate (§7) requires live matching evidence only at the moment of promotion, not afterward.

**One finding worth flagging (Medium):** the live-gate in `tabletopTrayLiveComponentMatches` requires not only a matching *promoted Library evidence item* but also that the *underlying raw* `tabletopShellResults`/`tabletopStorageResults` record still exists (`state.tabletopShellResults.find(item=>item.id===match.evidence?.fitContext?.sourceShellResultId)`). This is **stricter** than the architecture review's phrasing ("a currently existing exact-matching T2 Shell-proven record and exact-matching T3A Storage Fit Coupon-proven record") and is inconsistent with the rest of the app's own stated philosophy that promoted Library evidence is independent of its raw source once promoted (the T2/T3A "Delete raw result" confirmation dialogs explicitly say *"Library Evidence and the Production Setting remain independent"* when a promoted raw record is deleted). **Practical consequence:** if Joe ever deletes his raw T2 shell result or raw T3A storage result for tidiness (an explicitly supported action elsewhere), he would be **unable to promote a new T3B tray result later** even though his T2/T3A Library evidence is fully intact and would otherwise satisfy every other requirement — the eligibility message would read "A current exact T2 Shell-proven raw result and Library evidence are required at promotion time," which is accurate to the code but stricter than necessary. This is intentional (explicitly tested by two dedicated fixture assertions) and does not corrupt data, but it is a real, user-facing gate that the architecture review did not clearly specify either way. **Does not block Joe's real result today** (his raw T2/T3A records are presumably still present), but is worth flagging before he adopts a habit of deleting raw records after promotion.
